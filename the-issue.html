The Issue

Understanding water quality and how you can help

The Basics

What Is Water Quality and Why Should You Care?

Water quality describes how clean and healthy water is for people, wildlife, and coastal ecosystems. When water quality declines, the effects ripple outward—impacting beaches, fishing, wildlife, tourism, and local communities.

A Unique Ecosystem

Why Sanibel's Water Is Special

Sanibel sits at the intersection of rivers, estuaries, and the Gulf of Mexico. This mix of freshwater and saltwater supports seagrass meadows, fish and shellfish, birds, dolphins, and manatees.

Healthy water keeps these systems in balance.

The Challenges

What Affects Water Quality?

Water quality is influenced by things we do not always see:

1

Runoff from streets, lawns, and parking lots

2

Excess nutrients from fertilizers and wastewater

3

Boat activity that disturbs seagrass

4

Changes in freshwater flow

Many of these pressures start far from the coast—which means solutions do too.

Small Choices. Real Impact.

Protecting water does not stop when your vacation ends. The same actions that help Sanibel's waters help rivers, lakes, and coastlines across the country.

Take Action

What You Can Do

At Home

  • Use fertilizer sparingly or not at all
  • Fix leaks and maintain plumbing and septic systems
  • Choose native plants that need less water and chemicals

In the Water

  • Avoid seagrass when boating or kayaking
  • Never dump waste or chemicals into waterways
  • Dispose of trash and fishing line properly

As a Visitor

  • Respect wildlife and sensitive habitats
  • Support conservation-minded businesses
  • Share what you have learned with friends and family
