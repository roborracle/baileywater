About

Understanding and protecting Southwest Florida's coastal waters

Our Mission

Bailey Water Institute

The Bailey Water Institute works to protect and improve the water quality of Sanibel, Captiva, and surrounding coastal waters. As part of the Sanibel Captiva Conservation Foundation, the Institute conducts scientific research, monitors water conditions, and shares clear, accessible information to help people understand how water systems work and how everyday choices affect the health of our coastal ecosystems.

How We Work

Our Approach

Monitoring and Research

Scientists track water conditions to understand what is happening and why.

Clear, Accessible Education

We translate complex science into information people can use and relate to.

Community Connection

We help residents and visitors see how local water issues connect to water challenges everywhere.

The Power of Knowledge

Why Education Matters

Education is one of the most powerful tools for protecting water. When people understand how water systems work and how their actions affect them, they are more likely to make choices that protect wildlife, preserve habitats, and keep waters healthy. By sharing knowledge, we are helping safeguard the birds, fish, and marine life that depend on clean water today while protecting these places for future generations to enjoy.

Our Community

Why This Matters to Our Community

The clear waters around Sanibel and Captiva do not happen by accident. They are shaped by daily choices made on land and in the water—by people who live here and people who visit.
